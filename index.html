Noughts and Crosses (Tic-Tac-Toe) Game Instructions

Objective

The goal of the game is to get three of your marks (either "X" or "O") in a row, column, or diagonal before your opponent does.

Setup

The game is played on a 3x3 grid. One player is "X" and the other is "O". Players take turns placing their mark in an empty square.

How to Play

You can use your mouse to enter "O" or "X" in the box. Decide who goes first (usually "X" starts). Players take turns placing their mark ("X" or "O") in any empty square. Winning the game: The first player to get three marks in a row (horizontally, vertically, or diagonally) wins. If all squares are filled and no player has won, the game is a draw. You can reset your game in the middle of you game by clicking on "Reset Game" button. If one player is win or game is draw then you can restart the game by clicking "start new game".

Winning Conditions

A player wins if they get three of their marks in a row in any of these patterns: ✅ Horizontally (e.g., top row: X X X) ✅ Vertically (e.g., middle column: O O O) ✅ Diagonally (e.g., top-left to bottom-right: X O X)

Game Over Scenarios

Win: One player gets three marks in a row. Draw: All squares are filled, but no player has three in a row.
